STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Document Number:  SOP-QA-CX-091
Title:            Customer Complaint Intake, Investigation, and MDR Reportability
                  Assessment for OrthoNav SmartRobotics and IGS Navigation Systems
Department:       Post-Market Surveillance / Quality Assurance
Effective Date:   2025-03-01
Revision:         D

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This procedure establishes the requirements for receiving, documenting,
investigating, and determining Medical Device Report (MDR) reportability for
customer complaints related to the Acme OrthoNav SmartRobotics Surgical System
and Acme Surgical Navigation System 2.0 (IGS). It ensures compliance with 21 CFR
Part 820.198 and 21 CFR Part 803 (Medical Device Reporting).

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. SCOPE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This SOP applies to all complaints received by Quality Assurance from any
source (phone, email, field service reports, sales representatives, health
care facilities, patients, or regulatory authorities) regarding:
  - Acme OrthoNav SmartRobotics System (PMA P150002 and supplements)
  - Acme Surgical Navigation System 2.0 (510(k) K201847)

A complaint is defined as: any written, electronic, or oral communication
that alleges deficiencies related to the identity, quality, durability,
reliability, safety, effectiveness, or performance of a device after it
is released for distribution (21 CFR 820.3(b)).

This SOP does not cover internal non-conformances (see SOP-QC-NCR-001)
or supplier complaints (see SOP-PUR-SUP-005). Pre-market feedback is
handled under SOP-QA-DFMEA-002.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. RESPONSIBILITIES
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Complaint Intake Coordinator (CIC):
  - Receives and logs all incoming complaints within 24 hours of receipt.
  - Completes the initial Complaint Record (Form QA-CX-091) in the
    complaint management system (CMS: Pilgrim SmartSolve).
  - Assigns complaint to the appropriate Product Line QA Engineer for
    investigation within 1 business day.
  - Sends acknowledgment to the complainant within 2 business days of receipt.

Product Line QA Engineer (QAE):
  - Conducts or coordinates the technical investigation.
  - Completes the MDR Reportability Assessment (Form QA-MDR-003) within
    5 business days of complaint assignment.
  - Notifies the QA Manager immediately if the complaint is potentially MDR-
    reportable (serious injury, malfunction with potential for serious injury,
    or death).
  - Closes complaint within 30 calendar days (or documents justified extension).

QA Manager:
  - Reviews all MDR Reportability Assessments.
  - Makes final MDR reportability determination.
  - Signs and submits MDR to FDA within regulatory timelines (see Section 8.4).
  - Approves this SOP and all revisions.
  - Monitors complaint closure metrics and initiates CAPA for trends per
    SOP-QA-CAPA-007.

Field Service Engineer (FSE):
  - Documents field observations and device condition in the field service
    report (FSR) and forwards to the CIC within 2 business days of site visit.

MDR-Reportable Event: Any event for which a manufacturer becomes aware that
one of its devices may have caused or contributed to a serious injury,
malfunction with potential for serious injury, or death (21 CFR 803.50(a)).

Malfunction: The failure of a device to meet its performance specifications
or otherwise perform as intended (21 CFR 803.3).

7. SAFETY PRECAUTIONS
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.1  Confidentiality
     - Complaint information involving patient identifiers is subject to HIPAA.
       Do not include patient name, date of birth, or other PHI in complaint
       records unless encrypted and transmitted per IT Security SOP-IT-SEC-003.
     - Patient-identifiable information in MDR submissions to FDA is handled
       per 21 CFR 803.9 (confidentiality of patient information).

7.2  Returned Device Handling
     - If a device is returned for investigation, route to the Incoming
       Inspection area under quarantine and initiate a non-conforming product
       tag per SOP-QC-NCR-001 before any disassembly or testing.
     - Do not repair or dispose of a returned device under investigation
       without written approval from the QA Manager.

8. PROCEDURE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 1 — Complaint Receipt and Logging (CIC, within 24 hours)
         1.1  All complaint contacts (phone, email, FSR, regulatory authority
              letter) are routed to the CIC mailbox: complaints@[company].com
              or extension 4400.
         1.2  CIC opens a new Complaint Record in Pilgrim SmartSolve
              (Form QA-CX-091) within 24 hours of receiving the contact.
         1.3  Record the following minimum data elements:
              a) Date of receipt and date of event (if known)
              b) Complainant name, facility, and contact information
              c) Device model, serial number, lot number, and software version
              d) Detailed description of the complaint as reported
              e) Patient information (de-identified per HIPAA, if applicable)
              f) Whether a serious injury or death is alleged (flag immediately
                 if yes; notify QA Manager within 1 hour)
         1.4  Assign a unique complaint number (auto-generated by SmartSolve).
         1.5  CIC sends written acknowledgment to complainant within 2 business
              days of receipt (template: EmailTemplate-ACKMT-001 in EDMS).

STEP 2 — Initial Triage and Assignment (CIC, within 1 business day)
         2.1  CIC reviews the complaint description and flags the primary
              device family: OrthoNav or IGS Navigation.
         2.2  Assign complaint to the applicable Product Line QAE in SmartSolve.
         2.3  If the complaint alleges serious injury, malfunction potentially
              causing serious injury, or patient death:
              - Flag complaint as "POTENTIAL MDR" in SmartSolve
              - Notify QA Manager and Product Line QAE by phone immediately
              - The 30-day MDR clock begins at complaint receipt date
              (Note: Five-day MDR clock begins if the event requires remedial
              action to prevent unreasonable risk; notify QA Manager to assess)

STEP 3 — Technical Investigation (QAE, within 15 calendar days of assignment)
         3.1  QAE reviews all available information: complaint description,
              FSR (if available), device service history, and prior complaints
              for the same device serial number in SmartSolve.
         3.2  If a returned device is available:
              a) Retrieve from quarantine; confirm condition before testing
              b) Perform functional testing per the applicable test protocol
                 (TP-MAK-FT-001 for OrthoNav; TP-IGS-FT-001 for Navigation)
              c) Document test results in the Complaint Record
         3.3  Conduct Root Cause Analysis (RCA) using at minimum a
              fishbone/Ishikawa diagram or 5-Why analysis. Document RCA
              findings in the Complaint Record.
         3.4  Determine probable root cause category from the following:
              - Operator/use error
              - Device malfunction
              - Software issue
              - Installation/setup error
              - Environmental factor
              - No defect found / unable to reproduce (NDF/UTR)

STEP 4 — MDR Reportability Assessment (QAE, within 5 business days of assignment)
         4.1  QAE completes Form QA-MDR-003 (MDR Reportability Assessment)
              using the decision tree in Appendix A of this SOP.
         4.2  Three-question MDR reportability screen (per 21 CFR 803.50):
              Q1: Did the device malfunction? (Y/N)
              Q2: If the malfunction recurred, could it cause or contribute
                  to a serious injury? Consult RMF-MAK-001 or RMF-IGS-001
                  for applicable hazard severity ratings. (Y/N)
              Q3: Did the device cause or contribute to a serious injury
                  or death? (Y/N)
              If Q3 = Yes OR (Q1 = Yes AND Q2 = Yes): complaint is MDR-reportable.
         4.3  QAE submits completed Form QA-MDR-003 to QA Manager for review.
         4.4  QA Manager reviews assessment and makes final determination:
              - MDR REQUIRED: QA Manager initiates MDR submission per
                SOP-QA-MDR-001 within 30 days of becoming aware of the event
                (or 5 days if a five-day report is triggered)
              - MDR NOT REQUIRED: Document rationale in Form QA-MDR-003;
                QA Manager signs and retains in Complaint Record

STEP 5 — Complaint Closure (QAE, within 30 calendar days)
         5.1  Confirm all investigation steps are complete; verify Form
              QA-MDR-003 is signed by QA Manager.
         5.2  Document corrective actions or recommendations in the
              Complaint Record (customer follow-up, field safety notice,
              labeling update, CAPA initiation, etc.).
         5.3  If a systemic issue is identified (same root cause in ≥3
              complaints within 12 months), initiate CAPA per SOP-QA-CAPA-007;
              link CAPA number to the Complaint Record in SmartSolve.
         5.4  QAE closes the complaint in SmartSolve. QA Manager reviews
              and approves closure.
         5.5  If the complaint cannot be closed within 30 days, QAE documents
              the justified reason for extension in SmartSolve and provides
              a revised target closure date (maximum extension: 60 additional
              calendar days without QA Director approval).

STEP 6 — Complainant Response
         6.1  Upon closure, CIC sends a final response letter to the
              complainant summarizing findings and corrective actions taken,
              unless the complainant is anonymous or has opted out of follow-up.
              (Template: EmailTemplate-CLOSURE-001 in EDMS.)
         6.2  Do not disclose proprietary investigation details, MDR
              submission status, or regulatory correspondence in complainant
              response letters without QA Manager approval.

9. DOCUMENTATION AND RECORDS
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Form QA-CX-091  Complaint Record in Pilgrim SmartSolve CMS.
                All fields must be completed before complaint closure.
                Retained per 21 CFR 820.198(f): minimum device useful life + 2
                years, not less than 5 years from date of manufacture.

Form QA-MDR-003 MDR Reportability Assessment.
                Completed for every complaint with a potential adverse event.
                Retained with Complaint Record (same retention period).

MDR Submission (MedWatch 3500A)
                Copy of submitted MDR filed in complaint record and in the
                MDR Submission Log (maintained by QA Manager per SOP-QA-MDR-001).
                Retained per 21 CFR 803.18: minimum 2 years from date of
                submission.

Acknowledgment and Closure Letters
                Filed in SmartSolve under the complaint record.

CAPA Records    Linked in SmartSolve; governed by SOP-QA-CAPA-007.
