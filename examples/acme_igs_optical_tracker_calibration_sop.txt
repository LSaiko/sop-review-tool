STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Document Number:  SOP-CAL-IGS-018
Title:            Calibration of Acme Surgical Navigation System Optical Tracking Camera
Department:       Biomedical Engineering / Metrology
Effective Date:   2024-09-01
Revision:         B

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This procedure establishes the calibration requirements for the Acme Surgical
Navigation System 2.0 Optical Tracking Camera (OTC) used in intraoperative
image-guided surgery (IGS) workflows. Calibration ensures positional accuracy
meets the ≤1.0 mm root mean square (RMS) spatial error specification required
for safe navigated surgical guidance.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. SCOPE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This SOP applies to all Acme Surgical Navigation System 2.0 Optical Tracking Cameras
(model NAV-OTC-200 series) in service at facilities under this institution's
biomedical engineering maintenance agreement. It covers initial calibration
on commissioning, periodic recalibration, and recalibration after any repair
or optical component replacement.

This SOP does not apply to:
  - Acme Surgical Navigation System 1.x legacy cameras (see SOP-CAL-IGS-009)
  - Fluoroscopic C-arm calibration (see SOP-CAL-RAD-004)
  - Software-only navigation upgrades

The Acme Surgical Navigation System 2.0 is classified as a Class II medical device
(510(k) K201847). Calibration activities must comply with 21 CFR 820.72.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. RESPONSIBILITIES
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Biomedical Engineer (BME):
  - Performs all calibration steps per this SOP.
  - Operates the Acme Surgical NavCalibrate software tool.
  - Completes and signs the Calibration Record (Form CAL-IGS-018).
  - Applies calibration label per Section 9.4.
  - Escalates out-of-tolerance findings to the Supervisor immediately.

BME Supervisor:
  - Reviews and countersigns Calibration Records.
  - Approves out-of-calibration disposition and initiates NCR if required.
  - Ensures calibration standards used in this procedure are maintained
    on an active calibration schedule per SOP-CAL-003.

QA Manager:
  - Approves this SOP and all revisions.
  - Monitors calibration compliance during internal audits.

7. SAFETY PRECAUTIONS AND PPE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.1  Required PPE
     - ESD wrist strap when handling electronic optical tracking components
     - Powder-free nitrile gloves when handling calibration reference spheres
       and reference frame retroreflective markers (skin oils degrade
       reflectivity and introduce measurement error)

7.2  Infrared (IR) Safety
     - The OTC emits Class 1M infrared radiation. Do not view the IR
       illuminators directly with optical instruments (magnifying lenses,
       binoculars). Unaided viewing is safe per IEC 60825-1.

7.3  Environmental Requirements for Valid Calibration
     - Calibration must be performed in a room free from direct sunlight
       and supplemental IR sources (heat lamps, surgical lights) that
       may interfere with the optical tracking field.
     - Ambient temperature: 18–24 °C. Record temperature at start and end
       of calibration on Form CAL-IGS-018.
     - Humidity: 30–70% RH. Record on Form CAL-IGS-018.
     - If conditions are outside range, postpone calibration and document
       the deferral reason.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. PROCEDURE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 1 — Pre-Calibration Environmental Check
         1.1  Measure and record ambient temperature and relative humidity
              on Form CAL-IGS-018, Section 1.
         1.2  Confirm conditions are within 18–24 °C and 30–70% RH.
              If out of range, stop and document; do not proceed.
         1.3  Verify the calibration room is free of direct sunlight
              and active IR heat sources within 3 m of the OTC.
         1.4  Allow the OTC to warm up for a minimum of 15 minutes
              after power-on before beginning calibration measurements.

STEP 2 — Equipment Verification
         2.1  Verify OTC serial number against Form CAL-IGS-018 header.
         2.2  Confirm all calibration reference standards (Section 6.1)
              have current, valid NIST-traceable calibration certificates.
              Record certificate numbers on Form CAL-IGS-018, Section 2.
         2.3  Confirm NavCalibrate software version is 3.1 or higher.
              Record version on Form CAL-IGS-018, Section 2.
         2.4  Confirm calibration laptop (BME-LAP-003) has not had any
              software updates since the last verified calibration run.
              If updated, notify BME Supervisor before proceeding.

STEP 3 — Physical Inspection of OTC
         3.1  Inspect OTC optical windows for cracks, scratches, or
              contamination. If damaged, remove from service and initiate
              NCR per SOP-QC-NCR-001.
         3.2  Verify OTC mounting is stable; check all mechanical fasteners
              are tight. Tighten to 2.0 N·m using calibrated torque driver
              if any are found loose; record on Form CAL-IGS-018.
         3.3  Inspect reference frame retroreflective markers for
              delamination or damage. Replace any damaged markers using
              Acme Surgical-approved replacement kit (Part No. NAV-MKR-010)
              before proceeding.

STEP 4 — Volumetric Accuracy Measurement (NavCalibrate Guided Procedure)
         4.1  Launch NavCalibrate v3.1 and select "OTC Volumetric Calibration."
         4.2  Mount the NavCalibration Reference Frame Kit on the OTC calibration
              stand at the standard 1.5 m working distance.
         4.3  Follow the on-screen NavCalibrate prompts to collect reference
              frame measurements at the following orientations:
              - 0° (neutral), ±15°, ±30° roll; 0°, +20°, -20° tilt
              (9 measurement positions total; software guides operator
              through each position with visual confirmation)
         4.4  Record the NavCalibrate-reported RMS spatial error for each
              position in Form CAL-IGS-018, Section 3 (Volumetric Accuracy).
         4.5  RMS spatial error must be ≤1.0 mm at all positions.
              Any position exceeding 1.0 mm = calibration failure.

STEP 5 — Point Accuracy Verification (Reference Sphere Test)
         5.1  Place the calibrated reference sphere (Cal Tag: REF-SPH-007)
              at five positions within the tracking volume:
              center, and four quadrant positions at 0.5 m offsets.
         5.2  Using NavCalibrate "Point Accuracy" mode, record the OTC's
              measured sphere center position at each location.
         5.3  Compare measured vs. certified sphere diameter; deviation must
              be ≤0.5 mm at all five positions.
         5.4  Record all measured values and deviations on Form CAL-IGS-018,
              Section 4 (Point Accuracy).

STEP 6 — Dimensional Reference Bar Verification
         6.1  Place the certified 300 mm reference bar at three positions
              within the working volume (near, center, far).
         6.2  Record OTC-measured bar length at each position via
              NavCalibrate "Length Verification" mode.
         6.3  Acceptance criterion: measured length = 300.0 mm ± 1.0 mm
              at all three positions.
         6.4  Record results on Form CAL-IGS-018, Section 5.

STEP 7 — Disposition
         7.1  If ALL criteria in Steps 4–6 are met:
              - Mark Form CAL-IGS-018 as PASS.
              - Generate the NavCalibrate calibration certificate (PDF);
                save to \\BME-SRV-01\IGS\Calibration\[SerialNo]\[Date]\.
              - Affix calibration label (Section 9.4) to OTC housing.
              - Record next calibration due date (12 months from today)
                on Form CAL-IGS-018 and in the BME asset management system.
         7.2  If ANY criterion fails:
              - Mark Form CAL-IGS-018 as FAIL.
              - Remove the OTC from service immediately; affix red
                "OUT OF SERVICE — DO NOT USE" label.
              - Notify BME Supervisor within 1 hour.
              - Initiate NCR per SOP-QC-NCR-001.
              - Assess impact: identify all procedures performed since the
                last passing calibration; document impact assessment on NCR.

STEP 8 — Documentation and Filing
         8.1  BME Supervisor countersigns Form CAL-IGS-018.
         8.2  File original signed Form in BME Calibration Binder (physical,
              Building 2, Room 214) and scan to EDMS under asset record.
         8.3  Retain Calibration Records per 21 CFR 820.180 (minimum
              device useful life + 2 years, not less than 5 years).

9.4 Calibration Label Specification
    Label must include:
    - "CALIBRATED" in bold (minimum 8-pt font)
    - OTC Serial Number
    - Calibration date (MM/DD/YYYY)
    - Next calibration due date (MM/DD/YYYY)
    - Calibrated by (initials)
    Labels are green with black print; sourced from approved supplier
    per SOP-CAL-003 Appendix A.
