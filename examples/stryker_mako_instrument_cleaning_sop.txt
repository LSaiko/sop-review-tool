STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Document Number:  SOP-CLEAN-MAK-033
Title:            Pre-Sterilization Cleaning of Mako SmartRobotics Reusable Instrument Set
Department:       Central Sterile Processing / Quality Operations
Effective Date:   2025-01-15
Revision:         A

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This procedure establishes the manual and automated cleaning requirements for
reusable Mako SmartRobotics surgical instruments prior to sterilization,
to remove biological soil, lubricant residue, and bone cement particulates
that could compromise sterility assurance or cause instrument malfunction
during robotic-assisted total knee and hip arthroplasty procedures.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. SCOPE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This SOP applies to the following reusable components of the Mako
SmartRobotics Instrument Set (Catalog No. 7,207-052 series):
  - Mako bone cutting burrs (reusable, titanium alloy, multiple sizes)
  - Burr shaft assemblies and locking collets
  - Robotic arm drape frame components (non-sterile barrier side)
  - Leg positioner clamps and adapters

This SOP does not apply to:
  - Single-use components (discard per SOP-WAS-001 after each case)
  - The robotic arm itself (see SOP-CLEAN-MAK-010: Mako System Surface Wipe-Down)
  - Implant trials or final implant components

All Mako SmartRobotics components addressed here are accessories to a
Class III medical device (PMA P150002). Cleaning validation was performed
per AAMI TIR30 and ISO 15883.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. RESPONSIBILITIES
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Central Sterile Processing Technician (CSP Tech):
  - Performs all manual cleaning steps and operates the automated washer-
    disinfector per this SOP.
  - Records all parameters on the Instrument Cleaning Log (Form CS-CL-033).
  - Rejects any instrument failing visual inspection; sets aside for QC review.

CSP Supervisor:
  - Reviews completed Cleaning Logs each shift.
  - Approves washer-disinfector cycle printouts and reconciles to Cleaning Log.
  - Initiates instrument rejection documentation per SOP-QC-NCR-001 for
    instruments failing inspection that cannot be cleaned to specification.

Biomedical Engineering (BME):
  - Performs monthly verification of washer-disinfector cycle parameters
    per SOP-CAL-WD-007 (Washer-Disinfector Qualification).
  - Escalates any washer-disinfector cycle deviation to QA.

QA Manager:
  - Approves this SOP and all revisions.
  - Reviews cleaning validation data upon SOP revision.

6. MATERIALS AND EQUIPMENT
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.1  Cleaning Agents (approved for Mako instruments per CVR-MAK-033-A)
     - Enzymatic detergent: Prolystica 2X Concentrate (diluted 1:64 in
       RO water, 45–55 °C), approved per IFU-MAK-INST-001, Section 6
     - Neutral pH instrument lubricant: Instrument Milk (applied post-clean,
       pre-sterilization; compatible with steam per AAMI ST79)
     - 70% Isopropyl Alcohol, USP Grade — for final wipe of locking collets
       prior to sterilization wrap

6.2  Automated Equipment
     - Getinge HS 66 Washer-Disinfector, Unit WD-03 (see SOP-CAL-WD-007
       for cycle qualification records)
     - Designated instrument trays for Mako burr sets (Tray IDs: MAK-TR-01
       through MAK-TR-08; labeled on tray)

6.3  Manual Cleaning Supplies
     - Soft-bristle nylon brush set, sizes 2 mm and 5 mm (lumen brushes)
       — dedicated to Mako instruments, color-coded RED
     - Non-metallic pick for cement particulate removal
     - Ultrasonic cleaner (if available): Branson 5800, Unit US-02
       (15-minute cycle at 40 kHz, enzymatic solution)
     - RO water rinse station (water quality: ≤5 µS/cm conductivity;
       verified daily per SOP-CAL-WD-007)
     - pH test strips (range 4–10)

6.4  PPE (see Section 7)

7. SAFETY PRECAUTIONS AND PPE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.1  Required PPE — Decontamination Zone (soiled side)
     - Heavy-duty butyl rubber gloves (NOT latex; resist enzymatic detergent)
     - Fluid-resistant gown or apron
     - Full face shield (surgical instruments may splash during scrubbing)
     - Surgical mask or N95 where aerosol generation is expected

7.2  Required PPE — Clean Assembly Zone
     - Powder-free nitrile gloves
     - Cleanroom gown or dedicated clean-side attire

7.3  Chemical Hazards
     - Enzymatic detergent (Prolystica) is an irritant. If eye contact occurs,
       flush immediately with water for 15 minutes; notify supervisor.
     - SDS for all cleaning agents posted at the decontamination station.
     - Discard diluted enzymatic solution after each 8-hour shift regardless
       of appearance; do not reuse from previous shift.

7.4  Sharps Safety
     - Mako bone cutting burrs have sharp cutting edges even after use.
     - Always handle burrs with the dedicated non-slip burr grip tool
       (Part No. MAK-TL-GRIP-001); never grasp bare cutting edge.
     - Report all sharps injuries immediately per SOP-SAF-010.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. PROCEDURE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 1 — Point-of-Use Pre-Treatment (OR, immediately after case)
         1.1  At the OR sterile field breakdown, remove gross bone cement
              and tissue debris from burrs using sterile water and gauze
              (point-of-use treatment; prevents drying of soil).
         1.2  Submerge instruments in the transport container pre-filled
              with enzymatic soak solution (Prolystica 1:64 in RO water)
              for transport to CSP. Do not allow instruments to dry.
         1.3  Complete the case ID field on the Instrument Cleaning Log
              (Form CS-CL-033) and send with instrument transport container.

STEP 2 — Receive and Sort (CSP Decontamination Zone)
         2.1  Confirm instrument set identity against the case ID on
              Form CS-CL-033. Note any missing components.
         2.2  Sort instruments into categories on the decontamination counter:
              - Burrs and collets (lumen-containing; require brush cleaning)
              - Frame components and clamps (solid surfaces)
         2.3  Inspect for gross damage (cracked shafts, deformed cutting edges,
              missing screws). Tag damaged instruments "HOLD — QC REVIEW" and
              set aside; document on Form CS-CL-033.

STEP 3 — Manual Pre-Clean (Enzymatic)
         3.1  Fill the decontamination sink with Prolystica 2X Concentrate
              diluted 1:64 in RO water at 45–55 °C. Verify temperature
              with the calibrated digital thermometer (if available); record
              on Form CS-CL-033. Verify solution pH is 6.0–8.0.
         3.2  Fully submerge instruments. Soak for minimum 10 minutes.
         3.3  Using the 2 mm lumen brush (red-handled, Mako-dedicated),
              scrub all lumen channels of burrs and collets with overlapping
              strokes (minimum 5 full passes per lumen). Flush lumens
              with syringe of enzymatic solution after brushing.
         3.4  Scrub all external surfaces, joints, and serrations with
              the 5 mm nylon brush. Work in the direction of the surface
              finish to avoid surface damage.
         3.5  Rinse all instruments under flowing RO water (minimum 1 minute
              per instrument; minimum 3 complete rinse passes).
         3.6  Verify no visible soil remains. If soil persists, repeat Step 3.

STEP 4 — Automated Washer-Disinfector Cycle
         4.1  Load instruments into designated Mako trays (MAK-TR-01 through
              MAK-TR-08) per the tray configuration guide posted on WD-03.
         4.2  Confirm WD-03 is loaded with the Mako Validated Cycle (Cycle 4).
         4.3  Run Cycle 4. Cycle parameters (per SOP-CAL-WD-007):
              - Pre-wash: cold RO water, 2 min
              - Wash: Prolystica 1:200, 55 °C, 5 min
              - Thermal rinse: 90 °C, A0 ≥ 600 (minimum 1 min at 90 °C)
              - Drying: 90 °C, 10 min
         4.4  Upon cycle completion, retrieve the cycle printout from WD-03.
              Verify A0 value ≥ 600 and all temperatures were achieved.
              If cycle fails to meet parameters, instruments are NOT clean;
              repeat from Step 3 and initiate WD deviation log.
         4.5  Record cycle number, A0 value, and pass/fail on Form CS-CL-033.

STEP 5 — Post-Wash Visual Inspection
         5.1  Under bright white light (minimum 1,000 lux), inspect all
              instruments for:
              a) Residual soil, discoloration, or water spots (reject if found)
              b) Surface damage — nicks, corrosion, cracks (reject if found)
              c) Lumen patency — pass a cleaning wire through each lumen
                 to confirm no blockage
         5.2  Instruments failing Step 5.1 inspection are tagged "REJECT —
              DO NOT STERILIZE" and documented on Form CS-CL-033.
              QC NCR is initiated per SOP-QC-NCR-001 for any rejected instrument.

STEP 6 — Lubrication and Final Wipe
         6.1  Apply Instrument Milk lubricant to all joints and moving parts
              of collet assemblies per manufacturer IFU (thin coating; wipe
              off excess with lint-free wipe).
         6.2  Wipe locking collet contact surfaces with 70% IPA to remove
              lubricant from sealing faces (IPA is compatible with
              subsequent steam sterilization).
         6.3  Allow surfaces to air-dry (minimum 5 minutes before packaging).

STEP 7 — Packaging and Transfer to Sterilization
         7.1  Package clean instruments in CSP-approved sterilization wrap
              per AAMI ST79 Section 8 requirements.
         7.2  Affix an internal chemical indicator (Class 1) and an external
              chemical indicator (Class 3) to each package.
         7.3  Label each package with: instrument name, tray ID, packed by
              (initials), pack date, and sterilization load number (pre-assigned
              by CSP Supervisor).
         7.4  Transfer to the sterilization queue; update Form CS-CL-033
              with package labels and transfer time.

STEP 8 — Documentation Completion
         8.1  CSP Tech completes and signs Form CS-CL-033.
         8.2  CSP Supervisor reviews, countersigns, and files the original
              Form in the CSP Log Binder for the date of processing.
         8.3  Scan Form and washer-disinfector printout to EDMS under the
              instrument set's DHR record.

9. DOCUMENTATION AND RECORDS
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Form CS-CL-033  Instrument Cleaning Log — completed for every cleaning event.
                Includes: Case ID, received instrument inventory, pre-clean
                observations, WD cycle number and A0 value, visual inspection
                results, disposition, technician and supervisor signatures.
                Retained per 21 CFR 820.180 (device useful life + 2 years).

WD-03 Cycle Printout
                Paper printout from Getinge HS 66 for each cycle.
                Attached to Form CS-CL-033 in the CSP Log Binder.

NCR (Form QC-NCR-001)
                Initiated for any instrument failing visual inspection
                or any WD cycle that does not meet validated parameters.

Cleaning Validation Report CVR-MAK-033-A
                On file in QA Records Room; reviewed upon each SOP revision.

                                   supplier-provided cleaning status
