STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Document Number:  SOP-QC-MAK-042
Title:            Incoming Inspection of Mako RIO Robotic Arm Subassembly Components
Department:       Quality Control / Receiving Inspection
Effective Date:   2024-06-01
Revision:         C

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This procedure defines the incoming inspection requirements for Mako RIO
Robotic Intelligent Orthopaedic (RIO) robotic arm subassembly components
received from contract manufacturers, to verify dimensional conformance,
surface integrity, and labeling accuracy prior to acceptance into inventory
and subsequent assembly into the Mako SmartRobotics surgical system.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. SCOPE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This SOP applies to all incoming lots of the following Mako RIO subassembly
components:
  - Robotic arm link castings (Part Nos. MK-ARM-101 through MK-ARM-106)
  - Force/torque sensor modules (Part No. MK-FTS-220)
  - End-effector coupling assemblies (Part No. MK-EFC-310)

It covers activities performed in Receiving Inspection Area RI-02, Building 3,
by QC Inspection Technicians and QC Engineers assigned to the Mako product line.
This SOP does not apply to software-only components or to field service
replacement parts (see SOP-QC-MAK-055).

The Mako RIO system is a Class III medical device (510(k) K163360; PMA
supplement P150002/S017). All inspection activities are subject to the highest
level of 21 CFR Part 820 scrutiny.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. RESPONSIBILITIES
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
QC Inspection Technician:
  - Performs all receiving inspection steps per this SOP.
  - Completes Incoming Inspection Record (Form QC-IIR-042) for each lot.
  - Places disposition tags on accepted and rejected containers.
  - Escalates any borderline or ambiguous findings to the QC Engineer.

QC Engineer (Mako Product Line):
  - Reviews and approves completed Incoming Inspection Records.
  - Makes final accept/reject disposition for borderline lots.
  - Initiates Non-Conformance Reports (NCRs) per SOP-QC-NCR-001 for
    rejected lots.
  - Authorizes any inspection deviations or concessions in writing.

QA Manager:
  - Approves this SOP and all revisions.
  - Reviews NCR trends quarterly and initiates CAPA when systemic issues
    are identified (SOP-QA-CAPA-007).

Receiving / Materials Management:
  - Quarantines incoming lots in the designated hold area until disposition
    is complete.
  - Does not release material to the production floor without a signed
    Incoming Inspection Record indicating ACCEPT disposition.

7. SAFETY PRECAUTIONS AND PPE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.1  Required PPE
     - Powder-free nitrile gloves (mandatory — bare hands contaminate
       precision surfaces and may cause corrosion on stainless steel)
     - Safety glasses when handling components with sharp edges (link castings)
     - Steel-toed shoes in the Receiving Inspection area at all times

7.2  Handling Precautions
     - Robotic arm link castings may weigh up to 8 kg. Use two-person lift
       technique or approved lifting fixture LFT-MK-001 for components >5 kg.
     - Force/torque sensor modules (MK-FTS-220) contain sensitive piezoelectric
       elements. Do NOT drop or subject to mechanical shock. Handle with both
       hands; place on foam-lined tray only.
     - Do not use metallic tools directly on finished surfaces. Use only
       plastic or rubber-tipped tools when repositioning components on the CMM.

7.3  Chemical Hazards
     - IPA used for surface prep is flammable. Keep away from ignition sources.
     - SDS for IPA is posted at the chemical storage station in RI-02.

8. PROCEDURE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 1 — Receiving and Quarantine Verification
         1.1  Upon receipt of a shipment, confirm that the shipment is placed
              in the designated Quarantine Hold area (yellow floor marking,
              RI-02 north wall) by Receiving personnel.
         1.2  Verify a red "HOLD / QUARANTINE" label is affixed to each pallet
              or container. If missing, affix immediately and notify the
              QC Engineer.
         1.3  Do not begin inspection until the lot is quarantined and a
              purchase order (PO) number is confirmed in the ERP system.

STEP 2 — Document and Lot Verification
         2.1  Retrieve the supplier's Certificate of Conformance (CoC) and
              packing slip from the shipment.
         2.2  Verify the following against the PO and CoC:
              a) Part number and revision level (must match PO exactly)
              b) Lot or batch number (record on IIR Form QC-IIR-042)
              c) Quantity received vs. quantity on PO (note any discrepancy)
              d) Supplier name and Approved Supplier List status
                 (verify against SOP-QC-MAK-010 Approved Supplier List)
         2.3  If any discrepancy is found in Step 2.2, place the lot on HOLD,
              contact the QC Engineer, and do not proceed until resolved.

STEP 3 — Sample Selection (per ANSI/ASQ Z1.4)
         3.1  Determine lot size (N) from the packing slip or physical count.
         3.2  Using ANSI/ASQ Z1.4, Inspection Level II, AQL 1.0 for critical
              dimensions and AQL 4.0 for visual/cosmetic attributes:
              - Look up the appropriate sample size code letter for lot size N.
              - Record the sample size (n) on Form QC-IIR-042.
         3.3  Select samples using systematic random sampling (every k-th unit,
              where k = N/n, rounded to the nearest integer). Samples must be
              drawn from throughout the lot, not only from the top layer.
         3.4  Label each sample with a temporary ID tag (e.g., S-01, S-02…)
              and record tag-to-serial-number mapping on Form QC-IIR-042.

STEP 4 — Visual and Surface Inspection
         4.1  Inspect each sample under diffuse white light (minimum 1,000 lux)
              for the following cosmetic defects. Record findings on
              Form QC-IIR-042 using the accept/reject criteria in
              CAC-MAK-042, Rev. C, Section 3 (Visual Criteria):
              a) Burrs, sharp edges, or machining marks on non-functional surfaces
              b) Corrosion, pitting, or discoloration inconsistent with
                 316L stainless specification MSPEC-SS316L-001
              c) Scratches, gouges, or dents exceeding allowable limits
                 (>0.1 mm depth on product-contact surfaces = reject)
              d) Incorrect, illegible, or missing part markings or lot codes
         4.2  For components with surface finish requirements per drawing
              (Ra ≤ 0.8 µm on product-contact surfaces), measure Ra using
              the Mitutoyo SJ-210 roughness tester.
              - Record Ra values on Form QC-IIR-042.
              - Any measurement exceeding the drawing tolerance = reject that
                sample.

STEP 5 — Dimensional Inspection (CMM)
         5.1  Transport selected samples to Metrology Lab M-01. Confirm
              CMM BSG-1142 calibration sticker is current before use.
         5.2  Retrieve the applicable engineering drawing (DWG-MAK-ARM-101-C
              or applicable series) from the controlled binder.
         5.3  Measure all features designated as "Critical" (△ symbol on
              drawing) and "Major" (● symbol) per the GD&T callouts using the
              established CMM program (file: MAK_ARM_101_Rev_C.prg).
         5.4  Record all measured values on Form QC-IIR-042, Section 4
              (Dimensional Data). Note drawing tolerance for each feature.
         5.5  Any single Critical feature outside tolerance = automatic lot
              reject. Any Major feature outside tolerance on two or more
              samples = lot reject (apply AQL 1.0 accept/reject numbers).

STEP 6 — Force/Torque Sensor Functional Check (MK-FTS-220 only)
         6.1  This step applies to Part No. MK-FTS-220 only. Skip for
              MK-ARM-101 through MK-ARM-106 and MK-EFC-310.
         6.2  Using calibrated digital force gauge (DFG-0031), apply a
              controlled axial load of 100 N ± 2 N to the sensor mounting
              face.
         6.3  Verify sensor output via the Mako bench-test interface
              (MBT-Tool v2.3) reads within ±1.5% of applied load.
         6.4  Record applied load, sensor output, and percent deviation on
              Form QC-IIR-042, Section 5.
         6.5  Any deviation >±1.5% = reject that sensor unit; apply AQL
              reject criteria across the sample set.

STEP 7 — Disposition Decision
         7.1  Compare all recorded inspection results against acceptance
              criteria in CAC-MAK-042, Rev. C.
         7.2  If the lot PASSES all criteria:
              - Mark Form QC-IIR-042 "ACCEPT."
              - Affix green "ACCEPTED — QC" label to each container.
              - Remove red HOLD labels.
              - QC Engineer signs Form QC-IIR-042 to authorize release.
              - Notify Receiving/Materials to transfer lot to approved stock.
         7.3  If the lot FAILS any criteria:
              - Mark Form QC-IIR-042 "REJECT."
              - Maintain red HOLD labels; do NOT remove material from
                quarantine area.
              - QC Engineer initiates NCR per SOP-QC-NCR-001 within 24 hours.
              - Rejected lots are dispositioned per NCR process
                (scrap, return to supplier, or DRB concession review).
         7.4  QC Engineer reviews and countersigns Form QC-IIR-042 for
              every lot regardless of disposition.

STEP 8 — Documentation Filing
         8.1  File original signed IIR Form QC-IIR-042 in the Incoming
              Inspection binder for the applicable lot in the QC Records
              Room (Building 3, Room 301).
         8.2  Scan and upload a copy to the Electronic Document Management
              System (EDMS) under the DHR folder for the applicable PO.
         8.3  Retain Incoming Inspection Records per 21 CFR 820.180:
              minimum of 2 years beyond the expected device useful life or
              the life of the device, whichever is longer.
