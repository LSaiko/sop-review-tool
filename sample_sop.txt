STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Document Number: SOP-CLEAN-007
Title: Equipment and Workspace Cleaning Procedure
Department: Manufacturing / Quality Operations
Effective Date: 2024-03-15

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This procedure establishes the steps required to clean manufacturing equipment
and workspaces in the production area to prevent cross-contamination between
product lots and maintain a controlled environment.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. SCOPE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This SOP applies to all production equipment (mixers, filling lines, conveyor
surfaces), benchtops, and floor areas within the Class 10,000 (ISO 7)
cleanroom suite on the second floor of Building A. It applies to all
Manufacturing Technicians and Line Supervisors assigned to the area.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. RESPONSIBILITIES
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Manufacturing Technician: Performs cleaning per this procedure at the
  required frequency; completes the Cleaning Log (Form QA-CL-001).
- Line Supervisor: Reviews completed Cleaning Logs; verifies cleaning was
  performed correctly before releasing the line for production.
- QA Manager: Approves this SOP and any revisions; conducts periodic audits
  of cleaning compliance.

6. MATERIALS AND EQUIPMENT
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.1 Cleaning Agents
    - 70% Isopropyl Alcohol (IPA), USP Grade (approved supplier list EQ-SUP-004)
    - Sterile water (WFI quality) for final rinse
    - Mfr-approved neutral detergent (Part No. CLN-DET-103) — diluted 1:50 in WFI

6.2 Equipment and Supplies
    - Cleanroom-compatible lint-free wipes (Class 10 rated)
    - Stainless steel bucket (dedicated, labeled "CLEANING USE ONLY")
    - Plastic scraper (non-metallic) for residue removal
    - pH test strips (range 4–10)

6.3 Calibrated Instruments
    - Temperature/humidity datalogger (Cal. due per SOP-CAL-003)

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. SAFETY PRECAUTIONS AND PPE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.1 Required PPE
    Wear the following PPE at all times during cleaning:
    - Nitrile gloves (double-glove for IPA operations)
    - Safety glasses or face shield
    - Cleanroom gown and booties (per SOP-GMP-001 gowning procedure)
    - N95 respirator if aerosol mist is generated

7.2 Chemical Hazards
    - IPA is flammable. Keep away from open flames and hot surfaces.
    - Ensure adequate ventilation; activate local exhaust if available.
    - In case of skin contact, flush with water for 15 minutes and notify supervisor.
    - SDS for all cleaning agents is posted at the chemical storage cabinet.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. PROCEDURE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 1  — Notify and Isolate
         1.1  Notify the Line Supervisor that cleaning is beginning.
         1.2  Affix a "CLEANING IN PROGRESS — DO NOT USE" sign on equipment.
         1.3  Power down and lock out / tag out (LOTO) the equipment per
              SOP-SAF-005 before contacting any electrical components.

STEP 2  — Remove Gross Contamination
         2.1  Using the plastic scraper, remove any visible product residue
              from surfaces. Collect debris in a labeled waste bag.
         2.2  Dispose of waste per SOP-WAS-002 (Waste Disposal Procedure).

STEP 3  — Detergent Wash
         3.1  Prepare detergent solution: add 20 mL of CLN-DET-103 to
              1,000 mL of WFI in the dedicated bucket.
         3.2  Verify pH of solution is 6.5–7.5 using pH test strip; record
              on Cleaning Log (Form QA-CL-001).
         3.3  Wipe all product-contact surfaces with detergent-dampened
              lint-free wipes using overlapping strokes, working from clean
              areas to dirty areas.
         3.4  Allow contact time of at least 5 minutes.

STEP 4  — Rinse
         4.1  Wipe all previously cleaned surfaces with fresh WFI-dampened
              wipes to remove detergent residue.
         4.2  Repeat rinse wipe twice.

STEP 5  — Disinfection with IPA
         5.1  Apply 70% IPA to a fresh lint-free wipe (do not spray directly
              onto equipment to avoid pooling near electrical components).
         5.2  Wipe all product-contact surfaces; allow a minimum contact time
              of 60 seconds before proceeding.
         5.3  Allow surfaces to air-dry completely before resuming production.

STEP 6  — Visual Inspection
         6.1  Inspect all surfaces under adequate lighting (minimum 500 lux)
              for visible residue, discoloration, or damage.
         6.2  If residue is visible, repeat Steps 3–5.
         6.3  Line Supervisor confirms visual acceptance and signs Cleaning Log.

STEP 7  — Documentation
         7.1  Complete all fields on Cleaning Log Form QA-CL-001, including:
              equipment ID, date, time start/end, cleaning agent lot numbers,
              technician initials, and supervisor sign-off.
         7.2  File the original Cleaning Log in the batch record for the
              subsequent production run.

STEP 8  — Release
         8.1  Remove the "CLEANING IN PROGRESS" sign.
         8.2  Line Supervisor signs the Batch Record to release the line
              for the next production operation.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. DOCUMENTATION AND RECORDS
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Cleaning Log, Form QA-CL-001: Completed by technician at each cleaning event.
  Retained in the device history record (DHR) for a minimum of 2 years beyond
  the device's expected useful life or as required by 21 CFR 820.180.
- Environmental Monitoring Data: Filed per SOP-ENV-002.
